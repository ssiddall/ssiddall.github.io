Some CSCL issues that I've encountered

  • anonymity facilitates critical over moral thinking
  • group think problems persist even on-line
  • who is the author/owner of collaborative work?
  • who owns the archive of a listerver that produced new insights?
  • collaborative work is "reviewed" by others during creation
  • on-line collaboration loses the power of rhetoric, persuasion
  • on-line collaboration skills are important to the liberally educated
  • source of opinions is important in evaluating statements
  • tone of voice and body language is lost on-line
  • on-line collaboration reduces gender as a factor in discussions, decisions
  • effective on-line collaboration is usually short, quippy
  • anonymity is defeated by our communication idiosyncracies
  • collaboration creates situations where no one takes responsibility
  • collaborative exercises are usually dominated by one participant
