Videoconferencing Equipment Specifications

Introduction

Denison University and Kenyon College will purchase two sets of new videoconferencing equipment. The two colleges plan to use this technology to enhance collaborative teaching, and to facilitate administrative meetings and resource sharing. We seek to install industry-standard systems which have potential to be upgraded and keep pace with changes in the industry, as well as provide the highest quality video and audio transmissions. In addition to collaborating among two or more fixed, videoconferencing classrooms at our institutions, and related small colleges, we want to be able to engage with other types of videoconferencing systems, including emerging desktop videoconferencing systems. We expect a high level of performance and compatibility. We want systems that have easy-to-use interfaces that minimize the skills and training required for effective use. Users of the systems at our campuses will include faculty (teaching, interdisciplinary meetings, etc.), administrators (interinstitutional meetings), board members (to reduce travel as needed), and students (for academic purposes). Denison and Kenyon are members of the Five Colleges of Ohio Consortium, and the Consortium of Liberal Arts Colleges (60 colleges, nationwide). We anticipate that at some future time, many of these colleges, including the College of Wooster, Ohio Wesleyan University and Oberlin College may join us in purchasing compatible videoconferencing systems.

Requirements and Specifications
At each site, we need:
  • One codec
  • Three cameras, two voice activated
  • One visual presenter (document camera)
  • Three monitors
  • 30 frames per second capability
  • Transmissions speeds no less than 384 kbps, and as high as 1.5 mbps (T1).
  • Vendor supplied support and training
  • Systems that are H.320 and H.323 compliant
It is our plan to interview vendors and see demonstrations of systems between 6/1- 6/15. We would like to have installations operational by 8/31/98.
