External and internal pressure to change

Changes in learning to come?
Learning takes place many places and times
Learning is not an isolated process
Students teach, faculty learn
One-approach-fits-all assumptions are out
Basis of classroom authority is changing
Lectures will endure but not dominate

Is technology part of the solution?
Enhances information access
Increases student motivation, engagement
Accommodates different ways of learning, knowing
Creates partnerships, opportunities to collaborate
Changes content through visualization, simulation
Promotes a new “balance of power” in classroom
Encourages new methods of assessment
Improves communication skills
Greater opportunities for complex, interdisciplinary study

Bottom Line
  The most significant benefits of information technology are not likely to be achieved without changes to our teaching

Pedagogical Goals
to learn to review and select resources carefully
to receive context from instructors, to change traditional relationships
to learn socially sometimes, independently others, through diverse means
to publish for audiences other than the instructor, heighten the significance of work
to learn to communicate with electronic tools
to combine analytical and aesthetic elements
to learn how to collaborate

Challenges to success
breaking out of linear thinking
giving up control over users’ approaches to material
balancing sizzle and substance
maintaining a web over time
developing reference strategies
scalable to larger classes, survey courses?

Keys to success
Browse, browse, browse!
Work together early to develop collaborative spirit
Divide labor on substantive areas and technical tasks
Allow sufficient time for final editing
Develop project guidelines to ensure site continuity
Prepare for new grading/assessment schemes
