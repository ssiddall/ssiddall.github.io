What Causes DQOs to Fail?
u Incomplete planning results in failed or
u unrealistic DQO goals.
l Sampling sites are more inaccessible than   expected, don’t provide expected type or quality of samples, or cost more to sample.
l Analytical methods have matrix effects (poor analyte recoveries, unexpected interferences).
l Costs of sampling & analysis are too high.
l Data doesn’t meet DQO requirements.
