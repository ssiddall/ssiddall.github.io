Change: friend or foe?
Change from within can be positive (motivation)
Change from without  can be resisted (pressure)

All kinds of pressures to change

increased competition from alternative providers
meeting needs with limited (declining) budgets

high costs of information, technology and expertise

Recommendations for a helpdesk
answer the phone - no VM
return calls quickly, even just to confirm
establish departmental contacts
follow-up
be consistent, esp on network problems
survey clients, collect data and measure work
keep helpdesk staff in the loop
show them you care, not what you know
specialize and refer if needed
service is a priority

Review your processes from the client’s perspective
walk through each step, start to finish
consider time consumed by each step
reduce average time
keep value-added steps  (VA = outcome is improved)

Recommendations for web-based support
web pages aren’t the only answer
plan the project with clear goals
designate a manager, with time to do the work
very special mix of skills - aesthetic, technical, interpersonal
get commitment from others
fully commit to one module at a time, take time for it all
technical staff should not drive the site
technical staff must help plan the site
watch out for requirement creep
test, test and test
browse and copy
balance substance with sizzle
gather client feedback in design, use
interactive forms demand follow-up!
Keep content timely, accurate (demands effort)

Recommendations for training programs
always teach in context if you can
explore first, then select and train
promote peer-to-peer training
students in new roles as faculty trainers?
Adhere to hardware and software standards
